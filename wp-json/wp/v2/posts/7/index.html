Photo-realistic re-animation of portrait videos using only an input video<\/strong><\/p>\n\n\n\n

Synthesizing and editing video portraits\u2014i.e., videos framed to show a person\u2019s head and upper body\u2014is an important problem in computer graphics, with applications in video editing and movie postproduction, visual effects, visual dubbing, virtual reality, and telepresence, among others.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

The problem of synthesizing a photo-realistic video portrait of a target actor that mimics the actions of a source actor\u2014and especially where the source and target actors can be different subjects\u2014is still an open problem.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

There hasn\u2019t been an approach that enables one to take full control of the rigid head pose, face expressions, and eye motion of the target actor; even face identity can be modified to some extent. Until now.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

In this post, I\u2019m going to review \u201cDeep Video Portraits\u201d<\/a>, which presents a novel approach that enables photo-realistic re-animation of portrait videos using only an input video.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

In this post, I\u2019ll cover two things: First, a short definition of a DeepFake. Second, an overview of the paper \u201cDeep Video Portraits\u201d in the words of the authors.<\/p>\n\n\n\n
